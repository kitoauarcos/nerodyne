How it works
Two versions

Vortex, with or without a hedge.

Vortex is a single rules based strategy. At each rebalance it evaluates a family of models and allocates to the most diversified, high conviction portfolio. It is offered in two versions: without a hedge, or with a defined put option overlay. All figures below are full sample backtests from 2012 to the present.

Vortex seeks the full return. Vortex Shield adds the put option overlay for downside protection.

Methodology and output

We disclose the output, not the method.

The methodology behind Vortex is proprietary to Nerodyne and is not disclosed. As is standard for systematic managers, what subscribers act on is the output: the positions the strategy holds, when they change, and the corresponding hedge. The underlying rules remain confidential.

The hedge

A defined put option overlay.

Vortex Shield holds the same portfolio with a permanent hedge: a standing ladder of index (QQQ) put options together with a ladder of protective puts on the individual holdings. It is the risk managed version, designed to reduce losses in a market decline at the cost of some return in calm periods.

The overlay behaves like insurance: a known cost in quiet years, with a convex payoff in a sharp decline. Across the backtest this materially changed the outcome in the two weakest markets. In 2022 the hedged version returned +32% against +3.5% unhedged and −18.6% for the S&P 500, and in the 2020 decline +31% against +11% unhedged. The trade off is lower returns in calm years and somewhat higher volatility.
